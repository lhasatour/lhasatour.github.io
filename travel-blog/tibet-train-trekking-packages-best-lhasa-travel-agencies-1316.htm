Tibet Train + Trekking Packages: Best Lhasa Travel Agencies

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Why Combine a Tibet Train Journey with Trekking?

There’s no better way to experience Tibet’s breathtaking landscapes than by combining a scenic train ride with an immersive trekking adventure. The Qinghai-Tibet Railway, the highest railway in the world, offers unparalleled views of snow-capped peaks, vast grasslands, and turquoise lakes. Once you arrive in Lhasa, trekking through remote valleys, ancient monasteries, and high-altitude trails brings you closer to Tibet’s spiritual and natural wonders.

Highlights of the Tibet Train Experience

  • Stunning Scenery: Pass through the Kunlun Mountains, Hoh Xil Nature Reserve, and the Tanggula Pass (5,072m).
  • Gradual Acclimatization: The slow ascent helps reduce altitude sickness risks.
  • Cultural Immersion: Interact with Tibetan locals and witness nomadic life along the route.

Must-Do Treks in Tibet

1. Everest Base Camp (EBC) Trek

  • Difficulty: Moderate to challenging.
  • Highlights: Views of the north face of Everest, Rongbuk Glacier, and Tibetan villages.
  • Best Time: April–June, September–October.

2. Ganden to Samye Trek

  • Difficulty: Moderate.
  • Highlights: Crosses the Shuga La Pass (5,250m), visits ancient monasteries.
  • Best Time: May–October.

3. Mount Kailash Kora

  • Difficulty: Strenuous (3-day trek at 4,700m+).
  • Highlights: Sacred to Buddhists, Hindus, Jains, and Bonpos.
  • Best Time: May–September.

Essential Tips for Booking Your Tibet Adventure

Permits and Regulations

  • Tibet Travel Permit (TTP): Mandatory for all foreign travelers; must be arranged through a licensed agency.
  • Military Permits: Required for restricted areas like Mount Kailash.

Packing List

  • Clothing: Layered outfits for fluctuating temperatures.
  • Gear: Sturdy hiking boots, UV sunglasses, and a reusable water bottle.
  • Health: Diamox (for altitude sickness) and a basic first-aid kit.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Always circumambulate stupas and mani stones clockwise.
  • Ask permission before photographing monks or locals.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re drawn to Tibet’s spiritual aura or its rugged landscapes, combining a train journey with trekking offers the ultimate adventure. By partnering with a reputable Lhasa travel agency, you’ll ensure a hassle-free trip filled with awe-inspiring moments. From the rhythmic chants of monks to the silence of the Himalayas, Tibet is a destination that stays with you long after you leave.
