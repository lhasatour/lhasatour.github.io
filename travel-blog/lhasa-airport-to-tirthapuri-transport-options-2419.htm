Lhasa Airport to Tirthapuri: Transport Options

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Traveling from Lhasa Airport to Tirthapuri is an adventure that takes you through some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the Himalayas. Whether you're a pilgrim seeking spiritual solace or an adventurer chasing stunning vistas, understanding your transport options is key to a smooth journey. Below, we break down the best ways to make this trip, along with insider tips to enhance your experience.

Why Tirthapuri?

Tirthapuri, a sacred site in Western Tibet, is renowned for its hot springs, monasteries, and deep spiritual significance. It’s a lesser-known gem compared to Mount Kailash but equally mesmerizing. The journey from Lhasa to Tirthapuri is long but rewarding, offering glimpses of remote Tibetan culture and untouched natural beauty.

Getting from Lhasa Airport to Lhasa City

Before heading to Tirthapuri, you’ll first need to get from Lhasa Gonggar Airport (LXA) to Lhasa city. Here’s how:

Airport Shuttle Bus

The most budget-friendly option, the shuttle bus runs frequently between the airport and Lhasa city. The ride takes about 1.5 hours and costs around 30-50 CNY. Buses drop passengers near the Potala Palace or Jokhang Temple, making it convenient for travelers.

Private Taxi

For more comfort, hire a private taxi. The fare ranges from 200-300 CNY, and the journey is faster (around 1 hour). Negotiate the price beforehand to avoid surprises.

Pre-Booked Private Transfers

Many hotels and tour operators offer airport pickups. This is ideal if you’re carrying heavy luggage or prefer a hassle-free arrival.

From Lhasa to Tirthapuri: Transport Options

Once in Lhasa, you have several ways to reach Tirthapuri. The distance is roughly 1,000 km, and the journey can take 2-3 days depending on your mode of transport.

Option 1: Private Vehicle (Best for Comfort & Flexibility)

Hiring a 4WD Land Cruiser with a driver is the most popular choice. The route passes through Shigatse, Saga, and Mount Kailash, offering incredible scenery.

  • Pros:
    • Flexible stops for photos and exploration.
    • Comfortable for long distances.
    • Driver knows the terrain and permits required.
  • Cons:
    • Expensive (around 8,000-12,000 CNY for a round trip).
    • Requires a Tibet Travel Permit and Aliens’ Travel Permit.

Option 2: Public Buses (Budget but Challenging)

Public buses run from Lhasa to Ali (Ngari), but Tirthapuri is not directly accessible. You’ll need to take a bus to Darchen or Ali and then hire a local taxi.

  • Pros:
    • Cheap (around 400-600 CNY for a one-way ticket).
  • Cons:
    • Long, uncomfortable rides with limited stops.
    • No guarantee of onward transport to Tirthapuri.

Option 3: Joining a Tour Group

Many agencies offer Lhasa to Mount Kailash & Tirthapuri group tours. These include transport, permits, and accommodation.

  • Pros:
    • Hassle-free with all logistics handled.
    • Meet fellow travelers.
  • Cons:
    • Less flexibility in itinerary.
    • Fixed departure dates.

Essential Travel Tips

Permits & Documentation

  • Tibet Travel Permit: Required for all foreign travelers, arranged through a tour operator.
  • Aliens’ Travel Permit (ATP): Needed for travel beyond Lhasa.
  • Military Permit: Sometimes required for remote areas like Ngari.

Best Time to Travel

  • May to October: Ideal weather with clear skies.
  • Winter (Nov-Apr): Roads may be blocked due to snow.

Packing Essentials

  • Warm clothing (temperatures drop drastically at night).
  • Sunscreen & sunglasses (high-altitude UV exposure is intense).
  • Portable oxygen (for altitude sickness prevention).

Alternative Routes & Side Trips

If you have extra time, consider these detours:

Stop at Lake Manasarovar

One of the world’s highest freshwater lakes, revered in Hinduism and Buddhism.

Visit Mount Kailash

A sacred peak for multiple religions, perfect for trekking or pilgrimage.

Explore Guge Kingdom Ruins

Ancient ruins near Ali, offering a glimpse into Tibet’s historical past.

Final Thoughts

The journey from Lhasa Airport to Tirthapuri is more than just a commute—it’s an expedition through Tibet’s soul. Whether you choose a rugged bus ride or a private 4WD adventure, each option brings its own rewards. Plan wisely, respect local customs, and prepare for an unforgettable Himalayan experience.
