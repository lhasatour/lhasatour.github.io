Beijing to Lhasa: How to Handle Jet Lag

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Traveling from Beijing to Lhasa is an adventure of a lifetime, but the journey comes with its challenges—especially jet lag. The high-altitude destination, combined with the time difference and long flights, can leave even the most seasoned travelers feeling exhausted. Here’s how to minimize jet lag and make the most of your Tibetan adventure.

Understanding Jet Lag on the Beijing-Lhasa Route

Jet lag occurs when your body’s internal clock (circadian rhythm) is out of sync with the local time at your destination. While Beijing and Lhasa share the same time zone (China Standard Time, CST), the real challenge comes from:

  • Altitude Shock: Lhasa sits at 3,656 meters (11,995 feet) above sea level, while Beijing is only 43 meters (141 feet) high. The sudden change can worsen fatigue.
  • Flight Duration: Direct flights take about 4-5 hours, but layovers can extend travel time significantly.
  • Reduced Oxygen Levels: Lower oxygen in Lhasa can amplify tiredness, making jet lag feel more intense.

Pre-Trip Adjustments

Gradually Shift Your Sleep Schedule

If possible, adjust your bedtime 1-2 hours earlier a few days before departure. This helps your body adapt to Lhasa’s sunrise and sunset times, which may feel different due to its western location in the time zone.

Hydrate and Eat Light

Dehydration worsens jet lag. Avoid alcohol and caffeine 24 hours before flying, and drink plenty of water. Opt for light, protein-rich meals to keep energy levels stable.

Consider Altitude Acclimatization Supplements

Some travelers take natural supplements like Rhodiola rosea or Ginkgo biloba to help with altitude adaptation. Consult a doctor before trying any new supplements.

During the Flight

Stay Hydrated and Move Around

Airplane cabins are extremely dry. Drink water regularly and avoid excessive coffee or alcohol. Stand up, stretch, and walk around to improve circulation.

Adjust Your Watch Immediately

Since Beijing and Lhasa share the same time zone, you won’t need to change your watch. Instead, focus on syncing your activities (eating, sleeping) with Lhasa’s daylight hours.

Sleep Strategically

If you’re taking an early morning flight, try to sleep on the plane to arrive refreshed. For evening flights, stay awake to align with Lhasa’s schedule.

Arrival in Lhasa

Take It Slow for the First 24 Hours

Lhasa’s altitude means your body needs time to adjust. Avoid strenuous activities, and don’t rush to visit the Potala Palace right away. Instead, take short walks around Barkhor Street to acclimate.

Follow Local Meal Times

Eating at regular local hours helps reset your internal clock. Try Tibetan butter tea (po cha) for energy, but avoid overeating heavy meals.

Get Sunlight Exposure

Natural light helps regulate your circadian rhythm. Spend time outside during daylight hours to signal to your body that it’s time to be awake.

Managing Altitude Sickness Alongside Jet Lag

Recognize the Symptoms

Altitude sickness can mimic or worsen jet lag symptoms:
- Headaches
- Nausea
- Dizziness
- Fatigue

Use Oxygen if Needed

Many hotels in Lhasa provide oxygen tanks or oxygen-enriched rooms. Don’t hesitate to use them if you feel unwell.

Drink Herbal Teas

Tibetan locals swear by herbal remedies like sea buckthorn tea or ginger-infused drinks to combat altitude discomfort.

Long-Term Adjustments for Extended Stays

Maintain a Consistent Sleep Routine

If staying in Tibet for more than a few days, stick to a fixed sleep schedule to fully overcome jet lag.

Stay Active (But Not Too Active)

Gentle yoga or short hikes can help with circulation and sleep quality, but avoid overexertion.

Monitor Your Health

Listen to your body. If symptoms persist beyond 2-3 days, consult a local doctor.

Bonus Tips for a Smooth Trip

  • Pack Smart: Bring melatonin (if approved by your doctor) to help regulate sleep.
  • Use Technology: Apps like Timeshifter can create personalized jet lag plans.
  • Embrace Local Culture: Engaging in relaxed activities (like meditation or visiting a monastery) can reduce stress and help your body adjust.

By following these strategies, you’ll be able to enjoy the breathtaking landscapes, rich culture, and spiritual wonders of Lhasa without letting jet lag hold you back. Safe travels!
