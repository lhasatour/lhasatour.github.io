Scenic Views on the Lhasa High-Speed Train Route

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The Lhasa High-Speed Train, often referred to as the "Sky Train," is one of the most breathtaking rail journeys in the world. Connecting Xining in Qinghai Province to Lhasa in Tibet, this engineering marvel takes travelers through some of the most stunning landscapes on the planet. From snow-capped peaks to vast grasslands, the views are nothing short of spectacular. Whether you're a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor, this route offers an unforgettable adventure.

The Journey Begins: Xining to Golmud

The Gateway to the Tibetan Plateau

The train departs from Xining, a city known for its mix of modern development and traditional Tibetan culture. As the train climbs in elevation, the scenery shifts from urban sprawl to rolling hills and eventually to the vast Qinghai Lake, the largest saltwater lake in China. The sight of the lake’s turquoise waters against the backdrop of distant mountains is a highlight of the early leg of the journey.

Crossing the Kunlun Mountains

After Golmud, the train ascends into the Kunlun Mountains, a range often called the "Spine of Asia." Here, the landscape becomes more rugged, with jagged peaks and deep valleys. If you’re lucky, you might spot wild yaks or Tibetan antelopes grazing in the distance. The train’s large windows ensure you won’t miss a moment of the dramatic vistas.

The Heart of the Plateau: Tanggula Pass

The Highest Railway Point in the World

At an elevation of 5,072 meters (16,640 feet), the Tanggula Pass is the highest point on the Lhasa High-Speed Train route—and the highest railway pass in the world. The air is thin here, and the landscape is dominated by permafrost and snowfields. The train’s oxygen-supplied cabins make the high-altitude journey comfortable, allowing passengers to focus on the awe-inspiring views.

The Source of the Yangtze River

Near the Tanggula Pass, the train skirts the headwaters of the Yangtze River, China’s longest river. The area is a pristine wilderness, with crystal-clear streams cutting through alpine meadows. It’s a reminder of how untouched much of the Tibetan Plateau remains.

Entering Tibet: Nagqu to Lhasa

The Vast Northern Tibet Grasslands

As the train descends from the Tanggula Pass, it enters the Northern Tibet Grasslands, a seemingly endless expanse of rolling hills dotted with nomadic tents and herds of yaks. This is one of the best places to witness traditional Tibetan pastoral life. The grasslands are especially stunning in summer when wildflowers blanket the landscape.

The Holy City of Lhasa

The final stretch of the journey brings travelers into Lhasa, the spiritual heart of Tibet. The approach to the city is dramatic, with the Potala Palace—the former winter residence of the Dalai Lama—rising majestically above the urban sprawl. Arriving by train offers a gradual introduction to the city’s unique blend of ancient traditions and modern influences.

Travel Tips for the Lhasa High-Speed Train

Best Time to Travel

The ideal time to take this journey is from May to October, when the weather is milder and the landscapes are at their most vibrant. Winter travel is possible but can be challenging due to snow and extreme cold.

What to Pack

  • Warm clothing (even in summer, temperatures can drop significantly at high altitudes)
  • Sunglasses and sunscreen (the sun is intense at high elevations)
  • A camera with extra batteries (you’ll want to capture every moment)
  • Snacks and water (though the train has dining cars, it’s good to have extras)

Altitude Considerations

The train is equipped with oxygen systems, but it’s still wise to acclimatize gradually. Spending a day or two in Xining before the journey can help reduce the risk of altitude sickness.

Why This Journey is a Must-Do

The Lhasa High-Speed Train isn’t just a mode of transportation—it’s an experience. The ever-changing landscapes, the glimpses of Tibetan culture, and the sheer engineering brilliance of the railway make it a bucket-list adventure. Whether you’re drawn by the spirituality of Lhasa or the raw beauty of the Tibetan Plateau, this journey promises memories that will last a lifetime.
