Lhasa Travel Permit for Overland Entry from Nepal

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Traveling to Tibet is a dream for many adventurers, and entering overland from Nepal adds an extra layer of excitement to the journey. However, one critical document stands between you and the breathtaking landscapes of Lhasa: the Lhasa Travel Permit. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about securing this permit, the best routes, and insider tips to make your Tibetan adventure unforgettable.

Why You Need a Lhasa Travel Permit

Tibet is a highly regulated region, and foreign travelers are required to obtain a Tibet Travel Permit (TTP) to enter. If you’re planning to cross into Tibet from Nepal, you’ll need additional documentation, including:

  • China Group Visa (issued by the Chinese Embassy in Kathmandu)
  • Tibet Travel Permit (arranged by a licensed Tibetan travel agency)
  • Alien’s Travel Permit (for visiting restricted areas beyond Lhasa)

Without these permits, you won’t be allowed to board flights or cross the border.

Step-by-Step Process to Get Your Permit

1. Book a Tour with a Licensed Agency

Independent travel in Tibet is not permitted for foreigners. You must book a guided tour through a licensed Tibetan travel agency, which will handle your permit application.

2. Apply for a China Group Visa in Kathmandu

Unlike regular Chinese visas, the China Group Visa is specific to travelers entering Tibet from Nepal. Here’s how to get it:
- Submit your passport, visa application form, and a copy of your Tibet Travel Permit (provided by your agency).
- Processing usually takes 2-4 business days.

3. Receive Your Tibet Travel Permit

Your agency will send the permit to you in Kathmandu before your departure. You’ll need it to:
- Board your flight to Lhasa (if flying).
- Cross the Zhangmu or Gyirong border (if traveling overland).

4. Prepare for the Journey

  • Best time to travel: April to October (avoid winter due to road closures).
  • Altitude sickness prevention: Spend a few days in Kathmandu or Lhasa to acclimatize.

Overland Routes from Nepal to Lhasa

The Friendship Highway: Kathmandu to Lhasa

This iconic route takes you through some of the world’s most stunning landscapes:
- Kathmandu to Gyirong (New Border Crossing): Scenic drive with fewer restrictions than the older Zhangmu route.
- Gyirong to Shigatse: Pass through small Tibetan villages and high-altitude lakes.
- Shigatse to Lhasa: The final stretch includes visits to the Tashilhunpo Monastery and Yamdrok Lake.

Alternative: The Everest Base Camp Detour

If you’re an adventure seeker, consider adding a detour to Tibetan Everest Base Camp (EBC). Your permits must include this specific destination.

Common Challenges & How to Overcome Them

Permit Delays

  • Solution: Apply at least 3-4 weeks in advance, especially during peak season (May-September).

Border Regulations Changes

  • Solution: Stay updated through your travel agency, as policies can shift unexpectedly.

Altitude Sickness

  • Solution: Carry Diamox, drink plenty of water, and avoid strenuous activity in the first 48 hours.

Must-Visit Spots in Lhasa

Potala Palace

The former winter residence of the Dalai Lama, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is a must-see.

Jokhang Temple

The spiritual heart of Tibet, where pilgrims from all over the region come to pray.

Barkhor Street

A bustling market where you can shop for Tibetan handicrafts and try local delicacies like yak butter tea.

Final Tips for a Smooth Journey

  • Carry multiple copies of your permits and passport.
  • Respect local customs (e.g., always walk clockwise around stupas).
  • Pack wisely—layered clothing is essential due to fluctuating temperatures.

With the right permits and preparation, your overland journey from Nepal to Lhasa will be an adventure of a lifetime. Safe travels!
