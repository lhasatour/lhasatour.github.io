Lhasa Airport to Tholing: How to Reach

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Tibet is a land of mysticism, breathtaking landscapes, and ancient monasteries. While Lhasa often steals the spotlight, the remote town of Tholing (also known as Zanda) in Western Tibet is a hidden treasure waiting to be explored. If you're planning an off-the-beaten-path adventure, here’s everything you need to know about traveling from Lhasa Airport to Tholing.

Why Tholing Should Be on Your Tibet Itinerary

Tholing is home to some of the most stunning and historically significant monasteries in Tibet, including the Tholing Monastery, which dates back to the 10th century. The surrounding landscapes of the Zanda County feature the otherworldly "Earth Forest"—a surreal terrain of eroded clay formations that look like something from another planet.

For travelers seeking solitude, cultural depth, and dramatic scenery, Tholing is a must-visit. But getting there requires careful planning.

Getting from Lhasa Airport to Tholing

Step 1: Arriving at Lhasa Gonggar Airport

Your journey begins at Lhasa Gonggar Airport (LXA), the primary gateway to Tibet. Most international travelers fly into Chengdu or Kathmandu before connecting to Lhasa.

  • Airport to Lhasa City:
    • Taxi: The fastest option (~1 hour, ~¥200-300).
    • Airport Shuttle: Cheaper but slower (~¥30, takes 1.5 hours).

Once in Lhasa, acclimatize for 2-3 days to avoid altitude sickness before heading to Tholing (elevation ~3,700m).

Step 2: Lhasa to Shigatse (Xigaze)

Tholing is in Ngari Prefecture, a remote western region. The most practical route is:

  1. Lhasa to Shigatse (~270km, 5-6 hours by road).
    • By Private Vehicle: Recommended for flexibility (~¥1,500-2,000).
    • Public Bus: Cheaper but less convenient (~¥100, departs early morning).

Shigatse is Tibet’s second-largest city and a good stop to restock supplies.

Step 3: Shigatse to Saga (or Lhatse)

From Shigatse, the journey becomes more rugged.

  • Shigatse to Saga (~450km, 8-10 hours).
    • The road follows the Friendship Highway (China-Nepal Highway) before branching west.
    • Expect rough terrain—4WD vehicles are essential.

Saga is a small town with basic guesthouses. Stay overnight to break up the long drive.

Step 4: Saga to Tholing (Zanda)

This is the most challenging leg (~500km, 10-12 hours).

  • Route Highlights:

    • Lake Manasarovar & Mount Kailash: If time allows, detour to these sacred sites.
    • Dramatic Landscapes: High-altitude deserts, snow-capped peaks, and the surreal Zanda Earth Forest.

  • Road Conditions:

    • Unpaved sections, river crossings, and steep mountain passes.
    • A permit is required for Ngari Prefecture—arrange through a travel agency.

Alternative Route: Lhasa to Tholing via Ali (Ngari Gunsa Airport)

For those who prefer to minimize road travel:

  1. Fly from Lhasa to Ngari Gunsa Airport (NGQ) (~2 hours).
    • Flights are limited (typically 1-2 per week).
  2. Drive from Ngari to Tholing (~250km, 5-6 hours).

This option saves time but is weather-dependent and more expensive.

Best Time to Visit Tholing

  • May to October: Ideal for travel—warmer temperatures and passable roads.
  • Winter (Nov-Apr): Extreme cold and snow may block routes.

Essential Travel Tips

Permits and Documentation

  • Tibet Travel Permit (TTP): Mandatory for all foreign travelers—arrange via a registered agency.
  • Aliens’ Travel Permit (ATP): Required for Ngari Prefecture.

Packing List

  • High-altitude gear: Warm layers, sturdy boots, sunglasses.
  • Oxygen canisters: Tholing’s elevation can be challenging.
  • Snacks & Water: Limited facilities en route.

Hiring a Local Guide/Driver

Given the remote and rugged terrain, a Tibetan driver-guide is highly recommended. They know the roads, permit checkpoints, and best stops for food and rest.

Exploring Tholing: What Not to Miss

Tholing Monastery

One of the oldest monasteries in Western Tibet, featuring exquisite murals and ancient Buddhist relics.

Zanda Earth Forest

A geological wonder resembling a Martian landscape—best explored at sunrise or sunset.

Tsaparang

The ruins of the ancient Guge Kingdom, a short drive from Tholing, offer a glimpse into Tibet’s lost civilizations.

Final Thoughts

The journey from Lhasa to Tholing is not for the faint-hearted, but the rewards—untouched landscapes, deep cultural heritage, and a true sense of adventure—make it unforgettable. Whether you choose the long overland route or a flight to Ngari, Tholing promises an experience unlike any other in Tibet.
